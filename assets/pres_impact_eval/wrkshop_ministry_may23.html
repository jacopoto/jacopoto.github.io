Evaluation Approach

Improving Secondary Education in Malawi (ISEM) II

The program has three Specific Objectives addressing areas of:

  1. Equitable access to secondary education;
  2. Quality of education;
  3. Governance and management of secondary education.

Our Focus: Quality in Education

Teachers training in various aspects:

  • Develop teacher competency and Continuous Professional Development (CPD) frameworks;
  • Develop subject-based CPD materials;
  • Support school and cluster CPDs;
  • Offer scholarships to under qualified and degree-holding teachers, prioritizing females.

What is program evaluation?

  • Systematic process of collecting and analyzing data to assess the effectiveness and efficiency of a program
  • Answer questions such as:
    • How well is the program implemented and delivered?
    • What are the outcomes and impacts of the program?
    • How can the program be improved or sustained?

Why do we need program evaluation?

  • Provides the evidence and information to inform policy decisions and improve program design
  • Helps to measure the progress and performance of the program against its intended goals and objectives
  • Helps to identify the strengths and weaknesses of the program
  • Helps to ensure accountability and transparency of the program to its stakeholders and beneficiaries

What is the Impact of Program?

Impact of a program is the comparison between:

  1. The outcome of the program;
  2. The same outcome if the program had NOT happened;

Challenges Finding “Impact”

we cannot observe the “counterfactual”:

  • Either a school got textbooks or did not
  • Either teachers were monitored or not
  • Either a school introduces smaller class size or not
  • Either a policy happened or not…

Solution…?

We “Mimic” the Counterfactual

  1. FIND a group of individuals that are probably like the counterfactual
  • “Quasi-Experimental” methods
  1. CREATE a group of individuals that are like the counterfactual
  • Controlled Randomized Experiment

Methods

  • Simple comparison of means: compare averages. Possible if schools are randomly allocated.
  • Difference in differences: compare changes over time, before and after intervention;
  • Instrumental variables: use variable that affects intervention but not outcome as instrument;
  • Regression discontinuity: use discontinuity in intervention assignment based on cutoff value;

Can We Apply Any of These Methods to ISEM 2?

Implementation: where

Data Collection

Key evaluation questions for the quality of education program

  • How does increasing resources for schools affect student learning outcomes and school quality?
  • How does teacher training in various aspects affect teacher competency and student learning outcomes?
  • How does providing scholarships to underqualified or prospective teachers affect teacher recruitment, retention, and diversity?

Student learning outcomes

  • Data on the quantity and quality of resources procured and distributed to schools, such as textbooks, furniture, lab equipment, etc.
  • Data on the student learning outcomes in different subjects or skills, measured by standardized or customized tests
  • Data on the school quality indicators, such as student attendance, dropout, retention, completion, transition, etc.
  • Data on student characteristics, such as age, gender, siblings, address

Teacher Competency

  • Data on the teacher training activities
  • Data on the teacher competency indicators, such as knowledge, skills, attitudes, behaviors, etc., measured by self-reports, observations, assessments, etc.

Teacher Recruitment, Retention, and Diversity

  • Data on the scholarship program details
  • Data on the teacher retention indicators, (turnover rate, attrition rate, absenteeism)
  • Data on the teacher characteristics, (gender, age, qualification)

How to Collect Data?

  • Administrative data: such as number of pupils and teachers per school, characteristics of pupils and teachers, teachers’ turnover, pupils’ attendance, etc.
  • Surveys: Questionnaires or interviews with students, teachers, parents, school administrators, or other stakeholders
  • Tests: Standardized or customized assessments of students’ learning outcomes or achievements in different subjects or skills
